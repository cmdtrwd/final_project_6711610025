Origin & Purpose

What is Tarot?

The tarot is a pack of playing cards, used from the mid-15th century in various parts of Europe. In the late 18th century, it evolved into a powerful tool for divination, cartomancy, and deep psychological reading.

Today, tarot is widely used as an introspective gateway. It helps individuals tap into their inner wisdom, reflect upon their lives, and find clarity illuminating past, present, and future circumstances.

The Major Arcana

22 archetypal cards representing significant life events, major themes, and profound spiritual lessons.

The Fool

New beginnings, raw potential, leap of faith.

The Magician

Manifestation, resourcefulness, willpower.

The High Priestess

Intuition, the subconscious, divine feminine.

The Minor Arcana

56 cards reflecting the trials, tribulations, and everyday occurrences. Divided into 4 elemental suits.

Wands

✦ Fire

Passion, action, inspiration, and vibrant energy. The spark of creativity.

Cups

✦ Water

Emotions, intuition, relationships, and deep flowing feelings.

Swords

✦ Air

Intellect, truth, conflict, action, and powerful communication.

Pentacles

✦ Earth

Material matters, health, wealth, career, and physical grounding.
